First-Degree Murder

An Analysis by a 7th Grader

The "Planned Out" Murder

  • It's when a person **plans** to kill someone and then **intentionally** does it.
  • It's not a sudden crime; the person has to think about it beforehand.
  • This is why it's seen as the most serious degree of murder.

What Makes it First-Degree?

1. Premeditation

The act was thought out or planned in advance, even for a short time.

2. Malice Aforethought

A fancy term for having the specific intent to kill someone.
